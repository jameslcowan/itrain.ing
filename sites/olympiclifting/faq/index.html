Getting started

What is olympiclift.ing?

olympiclift.ing is a free Olympic lifting program builder you use in your browser. Plan your weeks, log the snatch and clean & jerk work, then share the whole plan as one open link — so lifters and coaches can spread real programming, not gatekeep it. No spreadsheet and no sign-up to start.

Is olympiclift.ing free?

Yes. olympiclift.ing lets you build, edit, and share programs for free. Paid extras like saved accounts or shorter links may come later, but the core builder is meant to stay free.

Do I need an account for olympiclift.ing?

No. Open olympiclift.ing, start typing, and share when you are ready. Logins for saving many programs are planned, but olympiclift.ing does not require an account today.

How do I make a Olympic lifting program on olympiclift.ing?

Open olympiclift.ing. You get a starter week. Name your training block, add days with Add Day, then fill in exercises, sets, reps, weight, % of max, and RPE. olympiclift.ing saves your work in the link as you type.

Does olympiclift.ing work on my phone?

Yes. olympiclift.ing is built for phones first. Turn your phone sideways if you want to see more columns at once.

Sharing your program

How do I share my program on olympiclift.ing?

Tap the share button at the top of olympiclift.ing. Your link is copied. Paste the link in a text, email, or DM like any other URL. More detail in how sharing works.

Can anyone with my link open my program on olympiclift.ing?

Yes — and that is the point. olympiclift.ing puts your full program in one link so anyone you share it with can open it instantly: an athlete, a training partner, a forum, or a lifter learning from your block. No login wall, no “request access.” olympiclift.ing is built to open up Olympic lifting knowledge. When paid plans launch, olympiclift.ing will offer private programs for coaches who want them; open one-link sharing stays the default for free use.

Does my olympiclift.ing link change when I edit my program?

Yes. olympiclift.ing updates the link as you type, so the link always matches what is on screen. Tap share again if you want to send a fresh copy after big changes.

Why is my olympiclift.ing share link so long?

olympiclift.ing stores your whole program inside the link. Bigger programs make longer links. Some chat apps cut off long links — try email, Notes, or a shorter program. Shorter links for olympiclift.ing are on the way.

I lost my olympiclift.ing link. Can you recover my program?

No. olympiclift.ing does not store your program on its servers. Save important links in your notes or bookmarks.

My shared olympiclift.ing program will not open. What should I try?
  • Ask for a new copy of the link — the message may have cut the link off.
  • Open the link in Safari or Chrome, not inside another app’s preview.
  • If the program is huge, try splitting the program into two blocks in olympiclift.ing.
Will old olympiclift.ing share links still work?

Yes. Older /p/… and #/p/… links still open in olympiclift.ing. New shares use /app/….

Building your program

How do I add weeks and training days on olympiclift.ing?

Use the week controls in olympiclift.ing to add weeks. On each week, tap Add Day (up to seven days). Delete a day with the trash icon on that day.

What is a mesocycle on olympiclift.ing?

On olympiclift.ing, a mesocycle is a training block — like a technique phase, strength block, or competition prep. Pick or rename the block from the mesocycle menu. Weeks belong to the block you have selected.

How do I set my the snatch and clean & jerk maxes on olympiclift.ing?

In olympiclift.ing, tap Config (the sliders icon). Enter your one-rep maxes for squat, bench, deadlift, and any custom lifts. olympiclift.ing can walk you through maxes the first time you open olympiclift.ing.

How do percentages and weight work on olympiclift.ing?

After you set a max in olympiclift.ing, typing a % of max fills in weight, and typing weight fills in the percent. RPE stays separate — you enter that yourself. See percentages and RPE for examples.

Can I use lbs or kg on olympiclift.ing?

Yes. Switch units in the week bar in olympiclift.ing. Your weights and maxes convert so the block stays consistent. Your olympiclift.ing share link keeps the units you picked.

Can I add power variations and other variations on olympiclift.ing?

Yes. Type any exercise name in olympiclift.ing. Names like power variations tie to your bench max. Add other lifts in Config if they need their own max.

For coaches

Can I send a olympiclift.ing program to an athlete?

Yes. Build the block in olympiclift.ing, share the link, and your athlete opens the link on phone or laptop. They see the same grid you built.

Can my athlete change a program on olympiclift.ing?

Anyone with the link can edit in olympiclift.ing in the browser. There is no view-only mode yet. Send a new link after review if you want a clean copy.

Is olympiclift.ing better than a spreadsheet?

For many coaches, olympiclift.ing is easier on mobile: one link, no formulas, and columns for sets, reps, %, RPE, and rest. Spreadsheets are still great if you need heavy custom math.

Your data

Where does olympiclift.ing store my program?

While you edit, olympiclift.ing keeps your program in the link and in your browser. olympiclift.ing does not keep a copy of anonymous programs on its servers today — your link is how the program travels.

Will my olympiclift.ing link show up on Google by itself?

olympiclift.ing does not auto-list individual shared program URLs in Google search — that keeps search focused on guides and templates, not random personal links. You can still post your link anywhere you want (social, forums, email, team chats) so people get the full program in one tap.

Is olympiclift.ing medical or coaching advice?

No. olympiclift.ing is a planning tool only. Talk to a coach or doctor about pain, injury, or what you should lift.

Coming soon

Does olympiclift.ing have free program templates?

Yes. Browse free templates on olympiclift.ing — technique weeks, snatch focus, meet prep, and more. Each opens in the same editor so you can edit and share your version.

Will olympiclift.ing let me save programs to an account?

Yes, that is planned so you can keep many programs in one place on olympiclift.ing. Open one-link sharing without an account will still work.

Will olympiclift.ing offer private programs?

Yes — planned for paid plans. Coaches and lifters who want password-protected or non-public blocks will get that option. Free olympiclift.ing will keep celebrating open links so programming knowledge stays easy to pass along.
